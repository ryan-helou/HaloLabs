# HaloLabs Strategy — what we NEED to do, what we COULD do, what we must NEVER do

*Synthesized 2026-07-02 from four research streams: (1) a first-hand r/QOVESStudio
customer thread, (2) a deep-dive on QOVES's product and reports (site, sample
report PDFs, reviews), (3) a competitor landscape sweep (Umax, LooksMax AI,
Mogged, RateByFresh, Maxxing, FaceIQ, brand skincare AI, human consults), and
(4) an evidence-and-risk review (perception science, dermatology, biometric
law, dysmorphia literature, press coverage).*

---

## 0. The one-paragraph thesis

The market is barbell-shaped. At the bottom: viral $4/week face-rating apps
(Umax, LooksMax AI) whose "advice" is one line of generica, whose scores change
on every scan of the same photo, and whose entire category is being written up
as a teen-dysmorphia machine by Fortune, The Conversation, and clinicians. At
the top: QOVES sells genuine specificity ($100–300, 521 landmarks, real written
analysis) but as a **static report with a 28-day wait, no product names, no
routine, no schedule, and no progress loop** — and its own customers' #1 recorded
request is *"just tell me where, how, and who — hold my hand."* Nobody credibly
owns the middle: **fast analysis → a prioritized, personalized plan with named
options, routines, costs and timelines → honest expectations → progress
tracking over weeks.** That middle is HaloLabs. We win by being the most
*actionable* and the most *trustworthy* product in the category — and trust is
built by the NEVER list as much as the NEED list.

## 1. What we NEED to do (table stakes + the differentiator)

### 1.1 The deliverable must be a PLAN, not a report (and never a score)

- **Lead with a plan, not a verdict.** QOVES's report takes ~1 hour to read and
  users love the depth — but what they *do* is ask support "ok but which cream?
  which laser? who do I go to?" Every finding must terminate in an action with:
  **what, why (tied to their photos), how (steps), what to buy (named product
  types + example brands), how often, what it costs, when they'll see results,
  and how strong the evidence is.**
- **Phase the plan.** Professionals stage interventions; so do we:
  - *Phase 1 — This week (quick wins):* neckline, brow tidy, part change,
    sleep schedule, SPF. Free-to-cheap, visible fast. Motivation fuel.
  - *Phase 2 — The next 90 days (habits & actives):* skincare actives
    (adapalene/BHA timelines), curl/hair routine, fat-loss target if relevant,
    minoxidil-class commitments with honest "reverses if you stop" flags.
  - *Phase 3 — Maintain & reassess:* re-photo, compare, adjust.
- **Daily routine view.** An explicit AM / PM / weekly schedule (dermatologist
  layering order: thin→thick, vitamin C AM, retinoid PM, never together,
  introduce actives one at a time). Nobody in the category renders an actual
  routine calendar; Neutrogena's 8-week regimen is the closest and it's a
  product funnel.
- **Shopping list.** The single most-requested missing feature in QOVES
  reviews. Name product *categories* with 1–2 example brands and price bands
  ("adapalene 0.1% — e.g. Differin, ~$15/mo"). Frame examples as examples, not
  endorsements; no affiliate links (see NEVER 3.7).

### 1.2 Analysis quality bar (what QOVES got right — copy it)

- **Annotated, feature-by-feature analysis.** Page/section per feature (hair,
  skin, brows, facial hair, eyes/under-eyes, smile, style, posture) with the
  observation stated *on the user's own photos* — QOVES's skin section marking
  wrinkles/spots directly on the face is the single most-praised detail vs
  "L'Oréal apps that just say 'you have wrinkles.'"
- **Personalization callbacks.** Users specifically cited "because of your
  work limitations, hairstyle X… can be swapped for Y" as the trust moment.
  Onboarding answers (time, budget, constraints, what they've tried, no-gos)
  must visibly shape the plan and be *quoted back* in it.
- **Strengths first.** QOVES's reassurance framing ("commented on my nose but
  not in a rude way, with the exact reason why") is why customers came back.
  Every analysis leads with what already works before what to change. This is
  also the BDD-aware framing clinicians recommend.
- **Rare-detail credibility.** "It picked up on my birthmark / my jaw's slight
  rotation — I've wondered for years" — noticing specific, true, non-obvious
  details is what separates "real analysis" from "AI slop." Better one precise
  observation than five generic ones.
- **Honest referrals build trust.** QOVES telling a user "filler can't fix an
  underbite — see a dentist" *increased* purchase intent. When something is
  out of scope (medical, structural), say so plainly and point to the right
  professional instead of stretching.

### 1.3 Evidence discipline (the recommendation engine's spine)

- **Tier every suggestion by evidence** and show it:
  - *Tier 1 (strong, always safe to push):* daily SPF (24% less photoaging,
    RCT), sleep (BMJ-published attractiveness effect), grooming/hygiene, body
    fat toward facial-BMI targets (visible at ~−2.4 BMI), teeth
    whitening/alignment, brow grooming, hairstyle, heavy-stubble default for
    patchy beards, posture.
  - *Tier 2 (works, needs commitment/expectations):* retinoids (12 wks),
    niacinamide/vit C, minoxidil/finasteride-class (reverses if stopped —
    always say so), cause-matched under-eye care (vascular→caffeine,
    pigment→topicals, hollow→"see a professional"), lash serums.
  - *Tier 3 (real but procedural — inform, never prescribe):* whitening
    in-office, aligners, professional treatments. Describe category + cost
    band + "consult a licensed provider."
- **Calibrate honestly.** Individual cosmetic effects are small (makeup ≈
  +0.25 points/level; identity dominates). Our pitch is the *stack* of many
  small reliable wins — say exactly that, in-product. Overpromising is both an
  FTC exposure and the fastest way to churn.
- **Triage by cause, not symptom.** Under-eyes, breakouts, dullness each have
  multiple causes with different fixes; the analysis must say *which* cause it
  sees before recommending.

### 1.4 Flow & mechanics (where every competitor leaks trust)

- **Guided capture.** Ask for specific shots like QOVES's ~6 (front neutral,
  front smile, both profiles, 3/4, detail shots) with lighting instructions and
  a checklist validator. Garbage in = generic out; capture quality is analysis
  quality.
- **Fast turnaround.** QOVES's 28-day (drifting to 52) wait is its most-hated
  attribute; our analysis takes minutes. Make the wait screen honest and short.
- **Consistency.** Same-photo-different-score is the #1 written complaint on
  Umax/LooksMax AI. We don't score at all — but the same photos must produce
  stable observations, and re-analysis must explain what changed and why.
- **Progress loop.** Check off suggestions, re-photo at checkpoints (weeks
  2/6/12 — matching real intervention timelines), compare against the
  baseline. The category's biggest structural gap; also our shareable artifact
  (progress delta, not a rating).
- **Privacy as a feature.** Local-only photos, nothing uploaded, no training on
  user images, delete = delete. Given Meta's $1.4B Texas settlement and the
  BIPA class-action wave, "your face never leaves your machine" is both our
  legal moat and a headline differentiator.

### 1.5 Tone (the entire brand lives here)

- Descriptive, kind, specific; suggestions framed as options ("if the
  patchiness bothers you, two routes…"). Tags (impact/effort/cost) describe
  the **intervention, never the person** — keep this rule sacred.
- Set expectations in-product: "three months of consistency," "this reverses
  if you stop," "this one is genetics — and it's fine."
- Reassurance section addressed head-on ("will this make me insecure?") —
  already on the landing page; keep it in the product too.

## 2. What we COULD do (roadmap options, roughly ordered)

- **Progress analytics.** Timeline view per person: photos over time, completed
  suggestions, "what changed since last analysis" diffs written by the skill.
- **Before/projection renders of the user** (already scaffolded via the
  `image` field): AI-edited "you after the plan" previews. High wow-factor —
  but see NEVER 3.4: keep them modest, grooming-only, clearly labeled
  projections, opt-in.
- **Video intake.** A slow head-turn video (frames extracted locally via
  ffmpeg) gives profile/3-4 views users fail to photograph well; also captures
  posture and expression in motion. Optional, never required.
- **Wardrobe/color analysis module.** Stylist-consult methodology (seasonal
  palette from skin tone, face-shape → glasses/hairline/neckline guidance) —
  image consultants charge £475+ for this; it's cheap for us to add.
- **Deeper onboarding** (the #1 QOVES user request): what they've tried,
  allergies/sensitivities, health flags to screen (their support promised
  this), personality/style identity, budget ceilings per category.
- **Specialized deep-dives** (QOVES's teased roadmap: nose report, hairline
  report): our equivalents as focused modules — "hair module," "skin module" —
  each with richer per-feature protocols.
- **Multi-person / household use** (already supported by the folder model) —
  couples doing plans together is an organic sharing loop.
- **Check-in nudges.** Local notifications/calendar export for routine
  adherence ("PM: adapalene night"). Adherence is where results actually
  happen.
- **Barber/derm handoff sheets.** A printable one-pager per suggestion to hand
  a barber ("curl-specific dry cut, light taper, keep length on top") or bring
  to a derm visit — bridges the "who do I go to" gap without us recommending
  specific clinics.
- **A "why we don't score faces" page.** Turn the NEVER list into public
  positioning — the anti-Umax stance is a marketing asset with journalists
  already primed by the Fortune narrative.

## 3. What we must NEVER do (each of these has a body count)

1. **Never output an attractiveness score, /10, percentile, tier, or ranking.**
   The score is the dysmorphia mechanism (Mount Sinai's Hildebrandt: "the more
   I get feedback that I'm not good enough, the more I consume it"), the incel
   taxonomy import (PSL scale = Puahate/Sluthate/Lookism), and the headline in
   every exposé. It is also Umax/LooksMax AI's #1 complaint (inconsistent
   scores). Our stat badges stay neutral counts; tags describe suggestions.
2. **Never itemize "flaws."** Observations are neutral and descriptive;
   advice is option-framed. QOVES's "flaw report" genre is literally cataloged
   in an AI-harms database (AIAAIC). The frame is "what the photos show,"
   never "what's wrong with you."
3. **Never recommend surgery, fillers, injections, or structural modification**
   — and never generate "post-surgery" morphs. This is QOVES's core criticism,
   the medical-advice boundary (unlicensed practice / FDA device territory),
   and the bright line between grooming coaching and body-modification
   marketing. When bone structure limits something: say it's structural, say
   it's normal, stop. Rx-adjacent topics (finasteride, tretinoin) are framed
   as "discuss with a doctor/dermatologist," with OTC alternatives named.
4. **Never oversell projections.** If we show "after" renders: grooming-level
   changes only, clearly labeled as illustrative projections, never altered
   bone structure, never a "your potential: 9.2" framing, and never tied to a
   purchase ("pay to see your best self" = exploiting insecurity, EU AI Act
   Art. 5 vulnerability-exploitation territory).
5. **Never process minors.** Hard 18+ gate at onboarding, stated policy, no
   teen-marketing funnel. COPPA's 2026 amendment adds face templates to
   personal info at $53k/violation; "apps that rate teen boys' faces" is the
   pre-written headline. Umax's 90%-male-16-45 funnel with an "18+" policy fig
   leaf is the exact gap a plaintiff's lawyer exploits.
6. **Never let face data leave the machine (or train on it).** No cloud
   uploads, no third-party face APIs, no training on user photos, published
   retention = user's own disk, delete = delete. BIPA/CUBI/GDPR Art. 9
   precedents run $35M (Snap) → $650M (Facebook) → $1.4B (Meta). Local-first
   isn't just architecture; it's the legal strategy.
7. **Never monetize insecurity mechanics.** No pay-to-reveal-your-analysis
   cliffhangers, no "unlock your true rating," no affiliate kickbacks steering
   recommendations (QOVES's stated reason for withholding product names is
   conflict-of-interest — our answer is examples-not-endorsements, disclosed),
   no fake urgency, no "invite 3 friends to unlock" dark patterns (Umax's
   referral unlock "frequently fails" into a charge — a chargeback machine).
8. **Never make "scientifically proven" claims we can't substantiate.** FTC's
   L'Oréal order and Operation AI Comply both apply: "grounded in published
   research" with citations = fine; "clinically proven to raise your
   attractiveness 2 points" = never. No accuracy claims for the analysis
   itself.
9. **Never ignore the wellbeing tripwires.** If a user's inputs signal
   appearance-related distress (BDD-pattern language in onboarding: "I can't
   leave the house," "I've checked this 100 times"), the product should gently
   surface support resources instead of a plan section. Validated screens
   exist (COPS ≥40 → refer); no competitor does this — its absence is "the
   category's most quotable indictment," and its presence is our clearest
   moral + PR differentiator.
10. **Never present the same advice to everyone.** Generic = the "it's just
    ChatGPT" review (MSPowerUser's verdict on LooksMax AI). If a suggestion
    could be pasted onto a random face, it doesn't ship. Every item must cite
    something visible in *their* photos or stated in *their* onboarding.
11. **Never rank or compare people** — no leaderboards, no "you vs average,"
    no celebrity-lookalike gimmicks. (Beauty.AI's all-white winners and
    FaceApp's "hot" filter are the canonical bias disasters; comparison
    features are where algorithmic bias becomes a headline.)
12. **Never bury honesty.** If photos are too poor to analyze, say so and ask
    for better ones (QOVES's photo validation). If something didn't work,
    the re-analysis says so. If tests/claims fail, no silent smoothing-over.

## 4. How this maps onto HaloLabs as built

| Research finding | HaloLabs implementation |
|---|---|
| Plan > report; "hold my hand" | `plan` object: phases, AM/PM/weekly routine, shopping list, checkpoints |
| Onboarding callbacks | `/start` wizard → `profile.json` → skill quotes it in `plan.summary` and suggestion `why` |
| Annotated per-feature analysis | Expanded observations + per-suggestion `why` tied to photos |
| Guided capture (QOVES's 6 shots) | `/start/photos` checklist: front neutral/smile, profiles, 3/4, detail; optional video |
| Fast turnaround | Local skill run in minutes; status screen instead of 28-day countdown |
| Progress loop | `progress.json` check-offs + re-photo checkpoints + `--force` re-analysis diffs |
| Privacy moat | Local-only stays; photos never leave disk; no cloud calls in viewer |
| Evidence discipline | `evidence` tier on suggestions; expectations strings; Tier-3 = "see a professional" |
| No scores / no flaws / no surgery | Already policy — now enforced in skill constraints + this doc |
| 18+ / wellbeing | Age gate in onboarding; support-resources note in the app footer |

*This document is the product constitution. When a feature idea conflicts with
section 3, section 3 wins.*
